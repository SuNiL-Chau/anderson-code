Now that you’ve reviewed the topline information about Anderson, your program and campus details, you may be wondering, “What’s next?” Here are the simple steps and an overview of what our team of professionals will connect you to. Our goal is to make this process as easy as possible, so you can save your energy for the thrill of enrolling and embarking on the next stage of your extraordinary career and life.

And the best part … I’ll give you a call during business hours and we’ll have a chance to speak directly.

4 STEPS TO GET YOU READY AND SET … TO GO

1

Connect with Us

Our Admissions Team will contact you during business hours, to answer any questions you have and set up a date and time that works for you for Step 2.

2

Confidential Interview

We’re excited to get to know you! When we meet, we will review your academic and career goals, discuss your options and determine your eligibility and the best program fit and course of action for you.

3

Financial Assessment

Whether you have been financially planning for school, setting money aside, or you just can’t seem to imagine how you would pay for college, not to worry, we have a team here to help. During our appointment, we will discuss the many and varied financial aid options. Financial assistance may be available for those who qualify.

4

Enrollment Ready!

Last but certainly not least, after all the meetings and deliberation, we did it! You are excited, comfortable with your chosen focus and field and it is now time to put pen to paper and enroll. Your Admissions Representative will take you step-by-step through all the forms to ensure you are ready to launch into the academic adventure ahead.

Funding Options

Financial assistance may be available to those who qualify.
Meet our Financial Aid Officers to learn more about your options:

Canada-Ontario Job Grant

The Canada-Ontario Job Grant (C.O.J.G.) provides an opportunity for employers to invest in their workforce with help from the government. If you’re an employer who needs to provide training for your employees, or an employee who wishes to inform your employer of the job grant and get training while employed, contact us to learn more. Applications are accepted on an ongoing basis and we will be happy to guide you through the process.

Windmill Microlending

Windmill Microlending, a registered charity since 2005, offers low-interest microloans of up to $15,000 to skilled immigrants and refugees to help obtain Canadian licensing or training required to work in your field.
